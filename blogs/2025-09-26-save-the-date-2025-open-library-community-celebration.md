<p>Each year since 2020, we’ve hosted a virtual celebration to honor the many global contributors who make the Open Library project possible and continuously improve the experience for our patrons.&nbsp;</p>



<p>This year’s Open Library Community Celebration will be held virtually on Tuesday, Nov. 4, at 9 a.m. PDT. </p>



<p>Volunteers, staff, patrons and friends of the library are invited to RSVP <a href="https://forms.gle/hPRY6AAt3mHCZqV59"><strong>here</strong></a> to get the link.</p>



<p>Last year was marked by more than <a href="https://blog.archive.org/2024/06/14/patrons-speak-out-the-impact-of-losing-access-to-more-than-500000-books/">500,000 books being removed from the library</a>,<a href="https://blog.archive.org/2024/05/28/internet-archive-and-the-wayback-machine-under-ddos-cyber-attack/"> cyber security attacks</a>, and power outages. Our <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1FdhNnT83uBMC6gjXl4w_VeYEyqXfnM6iwGgvYg9Wl0A/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.n0o9aed9lyeh">response</a> has been to focus on doing more with less: making the books we have more useful, making our contributors more effective, and targeting our efforts to the underserved communities who rely on our services most.</p>
